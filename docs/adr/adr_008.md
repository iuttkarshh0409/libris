# ADR-008: Adopt Evidence-First Response Generation

## Status

Accepted

---

## Context

The primary objective of Libris is to assist students in navigating academic textbooks while preserving trust, transparency, and academic integrity.

Large Language Models possess extensive pre-trained knowledge and are capable of generating fluent responses even when no supporting evidence is provided.

While useful in general-purpose conversational systems, this behavior conflicts with the goals of an academic reference platform.

The platform requires every factual response to be grounded in verifiable textbook evidence.

Response generation must therefore prioritize evidence over model intuition.

---

## Decision

The platform shall adopt an **Evidence-First Response Generation** strategy.

Every generated response must originate from evidence retrieved from the Knowledge Index.

Language models shall function exclusively as reasoning and communication components.

They shall not serve as independent knowledge sources.

Generation without successful retrieval is prohibited.

If sufficient supporting evidence cannot be retrieved, the platform shall explicitly communicate that limitation rather than attempting to generate an answer.

---

## Rationale

### Trustworthiness

Students should be able to trust every factual statement generated by the platform.

Trust is achieved through traceable evidence rather than model confidence.

---

### Academic Integrity

The platform is intended to support textbook-based learning.

Responses should encourage students to revisit original sources instead of replacing them.

---

### Explainability

Every generated statement should be traceable back to retrieved textbook passages.

Users should understand not only the answer, but also why the answer was produced.

---

### Hallucination Reduction

Restricting generation to retrieved evidence significantly reduces unsupported claims.

The platform intentionally favors admitting uncertainty over generating plausible but incorrect information.

---

### Architectural Consistency

This decision reinforces the separation between:

- Retrieval
- Grounding
- Generation
- Citation

Each Engine contributes a distinct responsibility without violating architectural boundaries.

---

## Evidence-First Workflow

```
User Question

↓

Retrieval Engine

↓

Retrieved Evidence

↓

Grounding Engine

↓

Prompt

↓

Generation Engine

↓

Raw Response

↓

Citation Engine

↓

Evidence-Backed Response
```

Evidence must always exist before generation begins.

---

## Consequences

### Positive

- Improved trustworthiness
- Reduced hallucinations
- Better citation quality
- Higher academic integrity
- Explainable responses
- Easier evaluation
- Better reproducibility

### Negative

- Questions outside the indexed textbooks cannot be answered.
- Response quality depends directly on retrieval quality.
- Missing or incomplete textbook coverage may result in unanswered questions.

These trade-offs are acceptable because the platform values correctness over completeness.

---

## Alternatives Considered

### Generation-First

Rejected.

Allowing the language model to answer before retrieval increases hallucination risk and weakens citation traceability.

---

### Hybrid Knowledge Generation

Rejected.

Combining textbook evidence with model pre-training knowledge makes it difficult to distinguish verified information from unsupported content.

This conflicts with the platform's educational philosophy.

---

### Internet-Augmented Generation

Rejected.

External knowledge sources violate the platform's defined knowledge boundary and reduce reproducibility.

---

### Optional Evidence

Rejected.

Making citations optional undermines user trust and weakens the platform's primary value proposition.

Evidence should remain mandatory.

---

## Implications

Every generated response must satisfy the following conditions:

- Retrieved evidence exists.
- Evidence is relevant.
- Every factual statement can be traced to retrieved content.
- Citations accompany the response.
- Unsupported claims are prohibited.

Generation should be considered unsuccessful if these conditions cannot be satisfied.

## Notes

Evidence-First Response Generation represents the core philosophical distinction between Libris and a general-purpose conversational AI system.

The language model's purpose is not to provide knowledge.

Its purpose is to organize, explain, and communicate evidence that has already been retrieved from trusted academic sources.

Whenever model intuition conflicts with retrieved evidence, retrieved evidence shall always take precedence.
